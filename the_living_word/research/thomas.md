# Lost Gospel: Thomas

**Title:** Gospel According to Thomas (Coptic: ⲡⲉⲩⲁⲅⲅⲉⲗⲓⲟⲛ ⲡⲕⲁⲧⲁ ⲑⲱⲙⲁⲥ)
**Tradition:** Nag Hammadi Library (Codex II,2)
**Date:** 50-140 CE (sayings possibly earlier)
**Original Language:** Greek (fragments) / Coptic (complete)
**Status:** Complete (114 sayings)

---

## Manuscript Evidence

| Witness | Date | Language | Contents |
|---------|------|----------|----------|
| Nag Hammadi II,2 | ~350 CE | Coptic | Complete (114 sayings) |
| Papyrus Oxyrhynchus 1 | ~200 CE | Greek | Sayings 26-33, 77 |
| Papyrus Oxyrhynchus 654 | ~200 CE | Greek | Prologue, sayings 1-7 |
| Papyrus Oxyrhynchus 655 | ~200 CE | Greek | Sayings 36-39 |

**Key Finding:** Three Greek fragments confirm early circulation independent of Nag Hammadi codex.

---

## Structure

**Prologue:** "These are the secret words which the living Jesus spoke..."

**Content:** 114 sayings (logia), no narrative framework
- ~50% parallel to synoptic gospels
- ~25% similar but modified
- ~25% unique to Thomas

**Organization:** Thematic collections, not chronological

---

## Key Sayings with Synoptic Parallels

### Saying 2: Seek and Find
**Thomas:** "Let him who seeks continue seeking until he finds..."
**Parallel:** Matthew 7:7-8; Luke 11:9-10
**Variant:** Thomas adds "when you find, you will be troubled"

### Saying 8: Fisherman
**Thomas:** "The man is like a wise fisherman..."
**Parallel:** Matthew 13:47-48
**Variant:** Similar but condensed

### Saying 9: Sower
**Thomas:** "The sower went out... some seed fell..."
**Parallel:** Mark 4:3-8; Matthew 13:3-9; Luke 8:5-8
**Variant:** No explanation (unlike synoptics)

### Saying 20: Mustard Seed
**Thomas:** "The kingdom is like a mustard seed..."
**Parallel:** Mark 4:30-32; Matthew 13:31-32
**Variant:** Thomas emphasizes seed's smallness

### Saying 26: Beam in Eye
**Thomas:** "You see the speck... but not the beam..."
**Parallel:** Matthew 7:3-5; Luke 6:41-42
**Variant:** Very close to synoptic form

### Saying 54: Beatitudes
**Thomas:** "Blessed are the poor..."
**Parallel:** Matthew 5:3; Luke 6:20-21
**Variant:** Shorter than Matthew

### Saying 71: Destroy Temple
**Thomas:** "I will destroy this house..."
**Parallel:** Mark 14:58; Matthew 26:61; John 2:19
**Variant:** Thomas: "no one can destroy it" (temple of body)

## Unique Sayings (Not in Synoptics)

### Saying 1: Secret Knowledge
"Whoever finds the interpretation of these sayings will not experience death."

**Analysis:** Gnostic-sounding, but may reflect early Jewish mystical tradition

### Saying 12: James the Just
"Go to James the Just... for whose sake heaven and earth came into being."

**Significance:** James' prominence matches early Jerusalem church
**Independence:** No synoptic parallel, possible historical value

### Saying 13: Three Statements
"I am not your master... you have drunk, you have become intoxicated..."

**Significance:** Peter vs. Thomas/Matthew comparison
**Parallel:** Some similarity to Matthew 16:17-19

### Saying 22: Male and Female
"When you make the two into one... male and female into a single one..."

**Significance:** Ascetic/Gnostic interpretation possible
**Resonance:** Galatians 3:28 (neither male nor female)

### Saying 114: Mary Magdalene
"Simon Peter said... Let Mary leave us... for women are not worthy of Life."
**Response:** "I myself shall lead her in order to make her male..."

**Significance:** 
- Contains: Male superiority assumption
- Response: Transformation to male = spiritual equality?
- Debated: Ascetic, Gnostic, or historical reflection

## Independence vs. Dependence Debate

### Case for Independence
1. **Greek fragments:** Early circulation (200 CE)
2. **No narrative framework:** Different from synoptics
3. **Order differences:** Sayings arranged thematically, not chronologically
4. **Form:** "Jesus said" rather than narrative context
5. **Simpler forms:** Some sayings lack elaboration in synoptics

### Case for Dependence
1. **Similar wording:** Close parallels to Matthew/Luke
2. **Combined sayings:** Some combine synoptic parallels
3. **Later date:** Nag Hammadi version ~350 CE

### Scholarly Consensus
**Most probable:** Independent stream of tradition (Q-like), not direct dependence on synoptics
**Confidence:** **Probable**

## Theological Themes

### 1. Secret Knowledge (Gnosticism?)
- Prologue: "secret words"
- Saying 1: interpretation prevents death
- Saying 62: hidden from wise/revealed to children

**Analysis:** Could reflect Jewish apocalyptic, not necessarily Gnostic

### 2. Kingdom Present
- Saying 3: "kingdom is inside you"
- Saying 113: "kingdom is spread out upon the earth"
**Parallel:** Luke 17:20-21

### 3. Light/Dualism
- Saying 61: "two will rest on a couch..."
- Saying 22: male/female unity
**Possible:** Platonic dualism OR Jewish apocalyptic

### 4. Asceticism
- Saying 27: "fast from the world"
- Saying 69: "blessed are hungry"
**Analysis:** Renunciation theme

## Translation Comparison

### Saying 3 (Kingdom Within)
**Coptic:** "The kingdom is inside of you, and it is outside of you."
**English:** "The kingdom is within you..."
**Interpretation:** Present reality, not just future

### Saying 77 (All-Encompassing)
**Coptic:** "I am the light... I am the All..."
**Significance:** High Christology OR Gnostic redeemer myth?
