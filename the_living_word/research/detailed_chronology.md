# Years of Life: Detailed Chronology

## Methodological Framework

**Sources:** Canonical Gospels, Pauline Epistles, Josephus, Roman History
**Dating Method:** Triangulation of:
1. Pontius Pilate's prefecture (26-36 CE)
2. Tiberius' reign (14-37 CE)
3. Luke 3:1-2 - "15th year of Tiberius" (~28/29 CE)
4. John's Passover references (multiple festivals)

---

## Year 30 CE: Baptism and Early Ministry

### Events
**Location:** Judea (Jordan River), then Galilee

**Chronology:**
- **Month 6 (Elul):** Birth of Yochanan (calculated)
- **Month 1 (Nisan):** Baptism of Yeshua (Luke 3:1-2)
  - "15th year of Tiberius" = 28/29 CE
  - Pilate as prefect
  - Caiaphas as high priest

**Duration in Judea:** ~40 days
- Temptation in wilderness (Mark 1:13; Matthew 4:1-11; Luke 4:1-13)
- Early disciples (John 1:19-51)
  - Andrew, Simon Peter
  - Philip, Nathanael

### Transition to Galilee
**John 1:43:** "The next day Jesus decided to go to Galilee"

**Significance:** 
- Mark 1:14-15 - "After Yochanan was arrested"
- Synoptics compress; John expands
- Different chronologies suggest source complexity

### Confidence: **Highly probable**
- Multiple attestation (all gospels)
- Historical anchors (Pilate, Caiaphas)

**Calling of Twelve:**
- Mark 3:13-19

### Thematic Development
- Conflict with Pharisees escalates
- Sabbath authority questioned
- Forgiveness of sins claimed
- Popularity grows

### Confidence: **Highly probable**

- Multiple attestation

**Mount of Transfiguration:**
- Mark 9:2-8
- 6 days after Peter's confession
- Elijah and Moses appearance

### Johannine Material
**John 5-6:**
- Healing at Bethesda (Jerusalem visit?)
- Bread of Life discourse (after feeding 5,000)

**Chronological Issue:**
- John places feeding 5,000 BEFORE passion

- Suggests: Multiple traditions or different events?

### Confidence: **Probable**
- Mark provides framework
- Some Johannine material difficult to place

## Year 32-33 CE: Final Journey and Passion

### Travel to Jerusalem
**Luke 9:51-19:27:** Journey narrative (Perean ministry)
- Samaritan village rejected
- Cost of discipleship teaching
- Mission of the 70/72
- Good Samaritan
- Lost sheep/coin/son
- Rich man and Lazarus
- Pharisee and tax collector

**Matthew 19-20:** Parallel journey material
- Divorce teaching
- Rich young ruler
- Laborers in vineyard
- Third passion prediction

**Mark 10:** Final approach
- Leaving Capernaum for Jerusalem
- Bartimaeus healing (Mark 10:46-52)

### Final Week

**Saturday:** Arrival at Bethany (John 12:1-8)
- Anointing at Bethany
- "Six days before Passover"

**Sunday:** Triumphal Entry (Mark 11:1-11)
- Palm branches, Hosanna
- Zechariah 9:9 fulfillment

**Monday:** Temple cleansing (Mark 11:15-19)
- Overturning tables
- "My house shall be called..."
- Cursing fig tree

**Tuesday:** Teaching in Temple (Mark 11:20-13:37)
- Authority questioned
- Parables (tenants, wedding feast)
- Tribute to Caesar
- Sadducees and resurrection
- Greatest commandment
- David's son/lord
- Widow's mite
- Olivet discourse (eschatology)

**Wednesday:** (John mentions "day before Passover")
- Anointing (in Mark/Matthew) OR second anointing?
- Judas' bargain (Mark 14:1-11)

**Thursday:** Last Supper (Mark 14:12-31)
- Passover meal
- Institution of Lord's Supper
- Betrayal prediction
- Gethsemane prayer and arrest

**Friday:** Trial and Crucifixion (Mark 15)
- Sanhedrin trial (night/early morning)
- Pilate trial
- Crucifixion (9 AM - 3 PM)
- Death at 3 PM
- Burial by Joseph of Arimathea

**Saturday:** Sabbath rest
- Women prepare spices (Luke 23:56)
- Guard at tomb (Matthew 28:11-15)

**Sunday:** Resurrection (Mark 16:1-8)
- Empty tomb discovered
- Appearances begin

### Confidence: **Directly attested**
- All four gospels narrate
- Paul confirms (1 Corinthians 15:3-8)
- Historical anchors solid

## The Missing Years: Unresolved Questions

### Question 1: Duration of Ministry
**One Year Theory (Synoptic):
- Mark implies one Passover
- Compressed timeline
- Ministry ~1 year

**Three Year Theory (John):
- John 2:13 - Passover (first)
- John 5:1 - Festival (unspecified)
- John 6:4 - Passover (second)
- John 10:22 - Hanukkah
- John 12:1 - Passover (third/final)

**Resolution Attempts:**
1. John contains historically accurate chronology
2. John's festivals = synoptic single year telescoped
3. Multiple visits to Jerusalem (synoptics omit)

**Confidence:** **Indeterminate** - Evidence insufficient to resolve

### Question 2: Chronological Gaps
**Synoptics:** 
- Baptism → Temptation → Galilee ministry
- Compressed, few specific dates

**John:**
- Baptism → Judea ministry → Samaritan mission → Galilee
- Multiple Jerusalem visits
- 2+ year ministry implied

**Gap Possibilities:**
- Time between baptism and Galilean ministry
- Early Judean activity (John 1-4)
- Return visits to Jerusalem

### Question 3: Historical Dating
**Birth:**
- Matthew/Luke: Herod the Great (d. 4 BCE)
- Luke 3:23: "about 30 years old" at baptism
- Suggests birth ~4-6 BCE

**Crucifixion:**
- 30 CE (John's three Passovers)
- 33 CE (synoptic one-year ministry)
- Lunar eclipse possibility (33 CE)

**Confidence:** **Probable** - 30-33 CE for crucifixion
