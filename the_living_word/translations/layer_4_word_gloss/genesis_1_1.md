# Genesis 1:1

## Layer 4: Word Gloss

```
[in] [beginning.of] [he-created] [Elohim/God] [DO] [the-heavens] [and-DO] [the-earth]
```

---

## Detailed Gloss

### בְּרֵאשִׁית - "in beginning of"

**Constituents:**
- **בְּ** - "in" (preposition indicating time/location)
- **רֵאשִׁית** - "beginning/head/start" (abstract noun from ראש "head")

**Semantic Range:**
1. "In the beginning" (absolute temporal) - traditional reading
2. "In the beginning of" (construct, relative) - "when God began to create"
3. "By way of beginning" (instrumental) - "by means of beginning"

**Ancient Translations:**
- **LXX:** ἐν ἀρχῇ - "in [the] beginning" (absolute)
- **Vulgate:** in principio - "in [the] beginning"
- **Targum:** בְּקַדְמִין - "in former time/at first"

**Confidence:** **Probable** - "In the beginning" (absolute) or "When God began" (construct)

---

### בָּרָא - "he created"

**Constituents:**
- **Root:** ב-ר-א (B-R-ʾ) - "to create"
- **Form:** Qal perfect 3ms

**Semantic Range:**
1. "Create" - bring into existence (especially by divine power)
2. "Fatten, fill" - possible etymological meaning

**Critical Distinction:**
- **בָּרָא** - divine-only verb (54x, always God as subject)
- **עָשָׂה** (asah) - "to make" (God or human subject)
- **יָצַר** (yatzar) - "to form" (God or human subject)

**Ancient Translations:**
- **LXX:** ἐποίησεν - "he made" (uses ποιέω, not κτίζω)
- **Vulgate:** creavit - "he created"
- **Targum:** בְּרָא - same root

**Confidence:** **Highly probable** - "he created" (divine-only verb)

---

### אֱלֹהִים - "Elohim/God/divine powers"

**Constituents:**
- **Root:** א-ל-ה (ʾ-L-H) - deity
- **Form:** Masculine plural

**Semantic Range:**
1. "God" - Israel's supreme deity (most common)
2. "Gods" - divine beings, other deities
3. "Divine council" - assembly of heavenly beings
4. "Angels/messengers" - heavenly beings (rare)

**Syntactic Note:** 
- Plural form with singular verb בָּרָא (he created)
- Traditionally: "plural of majesty" or intensive plural
- Critical scholarship: may reflect divine council imagery

**Ancient Translations:**
- **LXX:** ὁ θεός - "God" (singular)
- **Vulgate:** Deus - "God" (singular)
- **Targum:** אֱלָהָא (elaha) - Aramaic equivalent

**Confidence:** **Probable** - Supreme deity, possible council connotation

**Note:** Modern translations flatten to "God"; recommended to preserve "Elohim"

---

### אֵת - "[direct object marker]"

**Constituents:**
- **Form:** Particle (untranslatable in English)
- **Function:** Marks accusative/direct object

**Usage:**
- Precedes definite direct objects
- Indicates specificity ("the" heavens, not just any heavens)
- Not used with indefinite objects

**Ancient Translations:**
- **LXX:** Not translated (Greek uses accusative case)
- **Vulgate:** Not translated (Latin uses accusative case)
- **Targum:** יָת (yat) - Aramaic equivalent

**Confidence:** **Directly attested** - DO marker, untranslatable

---

### הַשָּׁמַיִם - "the heavens"

**Constituents:**
- **הַ** - definite article "the"
- **שָּׁמַיִם** - noun "heavens/sky" (masculine dual)

**Semantic Range:**
1. "Heavens" - sky, celestial realm
2. "Sky" - atmosphere, firmament
3. "Cosmos" - everything above earth

**Form Note:** Dual number (pluralis excellentiae) suggesting "upper and lower heavens"

**Ancient Translations:**
- **LXX:** τὸν οὐρανόν - "the heaven"
- **Vulgate:** caelum - "heaven"
- **Targum:** שְׁמַיָא (shemaya) - Aramaic

**Confidence:** **Highly probable** - "the heavens"

---

### וְאֵת - "and [DO marker]"

**Constituents:**
- **וְ** - conjunction "and"
- **אֵת** - direct object marker

**Function:** Coordinates second direct object with first

**Translation:** "and" (DO marker not visible in English)

**Confidence:** **Directly attested**

---

### הָאָרֶץ - "the earth/land"

**Constituents:**
- **הָ** - definite article "the" (with qamets before guttural)
- **אָרֶץ** - noun "earth/land" (feminine singular)

**Semantic Range:**
1. "Earth" - world, globe (modern understanding)
2. "Land" - ground, soil, territory
3. "Earth" as opposite of heavens

**Ancient Conceptions:**
- Flat earth with dome (firmament)
- Cosmic waters above and below
- Dry land vs. sea

**Ancient Translations:**
- **LXX:** τὴν γῆν - "the earth/land"
- **Vulgate:** terram - "earth"
- **Targum:** אַרְעָא (ar'a) - Aramaic

**Confidence:** **Highly probable** - "the earth" or "the land"
