# Genesis 1:1

## Layer 6: Literal Translation

```
In the beginning, Elohim created the heavens and the earth.
```

---

## Translation Philosophy

**Goal:** Produce readable English that:
1. Follows Hebrew word order where possible
2. Preserves key theological terms
3. Indicates textual ambiguities
4. Avoids interpretive additions

---

## Line-by-Line Translation

### בְּרֵאשִׁית → "In the beginning"

**Options considered:**
- "In the beginning" - Traditional, absolute temporal
- "When God began to create" - Construct reading (NJPS)
- "By way of beginning" - Instrumental

**Decision:** "In the beginning" - Most widely attested, preserves ambiguity

**Note:** Construct reading is plausible but not certain. MT vocalization favors absolute.

---

### בָּרָא אֱלֹהִים → "Elohim created"

**Critical choice:** Elohim vs. "God"

**Why "Elohim":**
1. Plural form is significant
2. Semantic range includes "divine powers/council"
3. Distinct from generic "God"
4. Later clarified by context

**Why "created":**
1. Divine-only verb (54x, always God as subject)
2. Distinct from "made" (asah) and "formed" (yatzar)
3. Implies bringing into existence

**Not:** "God made" - would lose specificity

---

### אֵת הַשָּׁמַיִם → "the heavens"

**Translation:** "the heavens"

**Notes:**
- Definite article preserved (ha- = "the")
- Plural maintained (heavens, not heaven)
- Merism component (heavens = above)

**Alternative:** "the sky" - Too narrow, loses cosmic scope
**Alternative:** "heaven" - Singular loses Hebrew plurality

---

### וְאֵת הָאָרֶץ → "and the earth"

**Translation:** "and the earth"

**Notes:**
- Simple conjunction "and"
- Definite article preserved
- "Earth" preferred over "land" for cosmic scope

**Merism:** "Heavens and earth" together = "the entire cosmos"
- Hebrew literary device: pair of opposites = totality
- Similar to "sun, moon, and stars" = all luminaries

---

## Alternative Literal Renderings

### Option A: Strictest Literal
```
In beginning, he-created Elohim the heavens and the earth.
```
*Too wooden, ungrammatical in English*

### Option B: Moderate Literal (Recommended)
```
In the beginning, Elohim created the heavens and the earth.
```
*Balanced: preserves Hebrew terms, readable English*

### Option C: Smoother Literal
```
In the beginning God created heaven and earth.
```
*Loses specificity: "God" for "Elohim", drops articles*

## Theological Implications

### What "Elohim created" implies:
1. **Divine agency** - Only God creates (bara is divine-only verb)
2. **Absolute beginning** - "In the beginning" suggests temporal starting point
3. **Cosmic scope** - "Heavens and earth" = everything
4. **Completed action** - Perfect tense suggests finished creation

### What this translation does NOT decide:
1. **Ex nihilo vs. ordering** - "Created" allows both interpretations
2. **Timeframe** - "In the beginning" doesn't specify duration
3. **Mechanism** - No description of how creation occurred
4. **Pre-creation state** - Doesn't address "waste and void" (Genesis 1:2)

**Original meaning:** **Highly probable**
