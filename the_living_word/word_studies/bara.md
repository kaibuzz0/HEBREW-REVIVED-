# Word Study: בָּרָא (bara)

**Lemma:** בָּרָא (bara)
**Root:** ב-ר-א (B-R-ʾ)
**Part of Speech:** Verb (Qal perfect, 3rd masculine singular)
**Occurrences:** 54x in Hebrew Bible

---

## Morphological Analysis

**Form:** בָּרָא
**Binyan:** Qal (simple active)
**Tense:** Perfect (completed action)
**Person:** 3rd person
**Gender:** Masculine
**Number:** singular

**Paradigm:**
- Perfect: בָּרָא (he created), בָּרָאתִי (I created)
- Imperfect: יִבְרָא (he will create), אֶבְרָא (I will create)
- Infinitive: לִבְרֹא (to create)

---

## Semantic Range

### Core Meanings

1. **"Create" (primary)** - to bring into existence
   - Genesis 1:1 - "God created the heavens and the earth"
   - Isaiah 42:5 - "Creator of the heavens"
   - Only used with divine subject (theological significance)

2. **"Fatten, fill" (possible root meaning)**
   - Connection to בַּר (fat, choice part)
   - Suggests "filling up" rather than creation ex nihilo

### Unique Characteristics

**Divine Use Only:**
- בָּרָא is used exclusively with God as subject
- Never used for human creation
- For human making: עָשָׂה (asah - to make/do), יָצַר (yatzar - to form)

**Comparison:**
| Hebrew | Meaning | Subject | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| בָּרָא | create | God only | Genesis 1:1 |
| עָשָׂה | make/do | God or human | Genesis 1:7, 2:15 |
| יָצַר | form/shape | God or human | Genesis 2:7, 2:19 |

## Historical Usage Timeline

### Biblical Hebrew
- Always with divine subject
- Cosmic creation (heaven, earth, sea)
- Creation of humanity (Genesis 1:27, 5:1-2)
- New creation (Isaiah 65:17 - "new heavens and new earth")

### Second Temple Period
- Expanded to include spiritual creation
- Psalm 51:12 - "Create in me a clean heart"
- 4 Ezra - new creation eschatology

### New Testament
- Greek: κτίζω (ktizo) - to create
- Romans 1:25 - "Creator" (κτίσας)
- 2 Corinthians 5:17 - "new creation" (καινὴ κτίσις)
- Ephesians 2:10 - "created in Christ Jesus"

## Ancient Translations

### Septuagint (LXX)
**ἐποίησεν** (epoiesen) in Genesis 1:1 - "he made"
- LXX uses ποιέω (poieo - to make) for בָּרָא
- Theological choice: less emphasis on creation ex nihilo?

**ἔκτισεν** (ektisen) - "he created"
- Used in Isaiah 42:5
- Closer to Hebrew concept

### Targum Onkelos
**בְּרָא** - Direct Aramaic equivalent
- Maintains Hebrew term
- Same theological restrictions

### Vulgate
**creavit** - "he created"
- Latin root of English "create"
- Jerome maintains Hebrew distinction

## Theological Interpretations

### Jewish Perspectives

**Classical Rabbinic:**
- **Creation ex nihilo debated:** 
  - Some: God created from pre-existing matter
  - Others: True creation from nothing
  - Genesis Rabbah: "In the beginning" can imply time or with wisdom

**Medieval:**
- **Maimonides:** Explicitly affirms creation ex nihilo
- **Nachmanides:** Torah begins with ב to show creation precedes all

**Modern Jewish:**
- NJPS: "When God began to create" - processive creation
- Emphasis on ongoing creation (tikkun olam)

### Christian Perspectives

**Patristic:**
- **Creation ex nihilo:** Affirmed at Nicaea (325 CE)
- **Christ as agent:** John 1:3, Hebrews 1:2
- **Augustine:** Time created with the world

**Reformation:**
- **Luther:** Emphasizes divine power
- **Calvin:** Immediate creation, no intermediate causes

**Modern:**
- **Concordist:** Science and Genesis compatible
- **Non-concordist:** Genesis is theological, not scientific
- **Framework view:** Days are literary structure

## Translation Decision

### Recommended Rendering

**Layer 6 (Literal):**
"he created" or "created"

**Layer 7 (Expanded):**
"he created [bara - divine creation verb, God-only subject, possibly: fattened/filled]"

### Rationale

1. **Preserve Hebrew specificity:** Don't flatten to generic "made"
2. **Indicate divine-only usage:** This is special vocabulary
3. **Note semantic debate:** Ex nihilo vs. ordering pre-existing
4. **Maintain distinction:** Separate from עָשָׂה and יָצַר

### Confidence Classification

**Original Meaning:** **Highly probable** - "he created" with divine-only subject restriction

**What Can Be Said with Confidence:**
- Unique divine verb
- Contrast with human "making"
- Cosmic scope (heaven, earth, sea, humanity)
- Ongoing use for "new creation" (eschatological)

**Debate Points:**
- Creation from nothing vs. ordering
- Single act vs. ongoing process
- Relationship to big bang/science

## Concept Connections

### Related Concepts

**חָדָשׁ (chadash) - new:**
- Isaiah 65:17 - "new heavens and new earth"
- 2 Corinthians 5:17 - "new creation"

**קָדַשׁ (kadash) - holy:**
- Creation = sanctification of time/space
- Exodus 20:11 - Sabbath grounded in creation

**רֵאשִׁית (reshit) - beginning:**
- Proverbs 8:22 - Wisdom as first of God's works
- John 1:1 - "In the beginning was the Word"

### Intertextual Network

**Genesis 1-2 Creation Accounts:**
- 1:1 - בָּרָא (bara) - cosmic creation
- 1:7 - עָשָׂה (asah) - making firmament
- 2:7 - יָצַר (yatzar) - forming man
- Three distinct verbs for three modes of divine action

**Isaiah 43:**
- "I create" (אֶבְרָא) new things
- Parallel to Exodus/new exodus
- Creation language for redemption
