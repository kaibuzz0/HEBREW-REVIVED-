# Word Study: אוֹר (or)

**Lemma:** אוֹר (or)
**Root:** א-ו-ר (ʾ-W-R)
**Part of Speech:** Noun (masculine) / Verb
**Occurrences:** ~45x in Hebrew Bible

---

## Morphological Analysis

**Forms in Genesis 1:**
- **אוֹר** (or) - noun, "light" (1:3, 1:4, 1:5, 1:18)
- **הָאוֹר** (ha'or) - "the light" (definite)
- **לְאוֹר** (le'or) - "for light" (prepositional)

**Parsing:**
- Noun: masculine singular
- Can function as subject, object, or in prepositional phrases

---

## Semantic Range

### Primary Meanings

1. **"Light" (physical)**
   - Illumination, brightness (opposite of darkness)
   - Genesis 1:3 - "Let there be light"
   - Daylight, sunlight

2. **"Light" (metaphorical)**
   - Knowledge, insight (opposite of ignorance)
   - Psalm 119:105 - "Your word is a lamp... light to my path"
   - Salvation, deliverance (Isaiah 42:6, 49:6)

3. **"Morning/Day" (temporal)**
   - "From morning to evening" (Nehemiah 8:3)
   - Daylight hours

4. **"Happiness, prosperity"**
   - "Light of your countenance" = favor/blessing
   - Ecclesiastes 11:7 - "Light is sweet"

### Root Development

**א-ו-ר (ʾ-W-R):**
- **אוֹר** (or) - noun: "light"
- **אוּר** (ur) - noun: "fire, flame"
- **אָרַר** (arar) - verb: "to curse" (different root!)
- **מְאוּרָה** (me'ura) - "luminary" (Genesis 1:14, 16)

**Important distinction:** 
- אוֹר (or) = light
- אוּר (ur) = fire
- Different vowel patterns distinguish meanings

## Genesis 1 Usage

### Day 1 - Light Created (1:3-5)

**וַיֹּאמֶר אֱלֹהִים יְהִי אוֹר וַיְהִי אוֹר**
"And God said, Let there be light, and there was light"

**Key observations:**
- אוֹר created **before** sun/moon/stars (Day 4)
- Light exists independently of luminaries
- God separates light from darkness (1:4)
- Names light "day" (1:5)

### Separation (1:4-5)

**וַיַּרְא אֱלֹהִים אֶת הָאוֹר כִּי טוֹב**
"And God saw the light, that it was good"

**וַיַּבְדֵּל אֱלֹהִים בֵּין הָאוֹר וּבֵין הַחֹשֶׁךְ**
"And God divided the light from the darkness"

### Later Usage

**מְאֹרֹת** (me'orot) - "luminaries" (1:14, 16)
- Light-givers (sun and moon)
- Different word: plural of מָאוֹר (ma'or)
- Created on Day 4 to "rule" the light

## Ancient Translations

### Septuagint (LXX)
**φῶς** (phos) - "light"

**Genesis 1:3-4:**
- καὶ εἶπεν ὁ θεός Γενηθήτω φῶς
- "And God said, Let there be light"
- καὶ ἐγένετο φῶς
- "And there was light"

**New Testament usage:**
- John 1:1-9 - "In him was life; and the life was the light of men"
- Light as divine revelation and Christ himself

### Vulgate
**lux** - "light"
- Latin root of English "luxury" (originally "brightness")
- Isaiah 9:2 - "lux magna" (great light)

### Targum Onkelos
**נְהוֹרָא** (nehōra) - Aramaic equivalent
- Used throughout Genesis 1

## Theological Interpretations

### Jewish Perspectives

**Mystical (Kabbalah):**
- אוֹר = primordial light (אוֹר הָאֵזֶר - or ha'ezel)
- Different from physical light of sun
- Spiritual illumination

**Rabbinic:**
- Light created before luminaries to show God's direct creative power
- Light = Torah (Proverbs 6:23 - "commandment is a lamp, Torah is light")
- Light = reward of righteous (future world)

**Messianic expectation:**
- Isaiah 60:1 - "Arise, shine; for your light has come"
- Isaiah 9:2 - "The people walking in darkness have seen a great light"

### Christian Perspectives

**Patristic:**
- **Origen:** Light = divine Logos, reason
- **Augustine:** Light = spiritual illumination
- Light created before sun shows spiritual before physical

**New Testament fulfillment:**
- John 1:4-9 - Christ as "the true Light"
- Matthew 4:16 - "the people living in darkness have seen a great light"
- 2 Corinthians 4:6 - "God made his light shine in our hearts"
- Revelation 21:23 - New Jerusalem needs no sun, Lamb is the light

**Typology:**
- Physical light (Genesis 1) → Spiritual light (Christ)
- "Let there be light" = spiritual creation/new birth
- Separation of light/darkness = judgment/division

### Light/Darkness Dualism

**Biblical development:**
- Not metaphysical dualism (like Zoroastrianism)
- Moral/spiritual dualism
- Light = God/good/truth
- Darkness = evil/falsehood

**John's Gospel:**
- Light vs. darkness (1:5)
- Walking in light (8:12)
- Judgment: light exposes darkness (3:19-21)

## Theological Significance

### In Genesis 1

1. **Light before luminaries** - God's power not dependent on sun
2. **Goodness of light** - explicitly "good" (1:4)
3. **Separation** - light/darkness division fundamental
4. **Naming** - light called "day" (time measurement begins)

### Eschatological

**Revelation 21-22:**
- No need for sun/moon in New Jerusalem
- Lamb is the light
- Nations walk by that light
- Fulfillment of Genesis 1: light returns to source (God)

### Anthropological

**Human as light-bearer:**
- Matthew 5:14 - "You are the light of the world"
- Reflects divine image (Genesis 1:26-27)
- Responsibility to illumine

## Translation Decision

### Recommended: "Light"

**Why not alternatives:**
- Not "sunlight" (no sun yet!)
- Not "day" (day is named later)
- Not "knowledge" (too interpretive)

**Preserve ambiguity:**
- Physical and spiritual dimensions
- Multiple layers of meaning
- Connection to later revelation

### Expanded translation:

**Genesis 1:3-4:**
"And God said, 'Let there be light' [or - illumination, physical and spiritual], and there was light. And God saw the light, that it was good; and God divided the light from the darkness."
