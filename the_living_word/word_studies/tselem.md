# Word Study: צֶלֶם (tselem)

**Lemma:** צֶלֶם (tselem)
**Root:** צ-ל-מ (Ṣ-L-M)
**Part of Speech:** Noun (masculine)
**Occurrences:** 17x in Hebrew Bible

---

## Morphological Analysis

**Form:** צֶלֶם (tselem) / צַלְמֵנוּ (tsalmenu - our image)

**Grammatical forms in Genesis 1:26-27:**
- **בְּצַלְמֵנוּ** (bǝ-tsalmenu) - "in our image" (1:26)
  - Preposition בְּ (in) + noun (tselam) + suffix נוּ (our)
  
- **כִּדְמוּתֵנוּ** (ki-demutenu) - "according to our likeness" (1:26)
  - Preposition כְּ (like/as) + noun (demut) + suffix נוּ (our)
  
- **בְּצַלְמוֹ** (bǝ-tsalmo) - "in his image" (1:27)
  - Preposition בְּ + noun + suffix וֹ (his)

**Parsing:**
- Singular noun with possessive suffixes
- Construct/absolute form varies by context

---

## Semantic Range

### Core Meanings

1. **"Image, likeness, statue"** (concrete)
   - Used for idols/statues (Numbers 33:52)
   - Physical representation of something

2. **"Image"** (abstract)
   - Genesis 1:26-27 - humanity as image of God
   - Psalm 73:20 - "image" in a dream (fleeting)

3. **"Form, shape"**
   - General appearance or representation

### Etymological Connections

**Related words:**
- **צֶלֶם** (tselem) - image
- **צַלְמָא** (tsalma) - Aramaic equivalent
- **צַל** (tsel) - shadow (connection debated)
- **מֵצַל** (metsal) - image (Ezekiel 43:15)

**Ugaritic connection:**
- Ugaritic ẓlm = "image, statue"
- Used in Baal cycle for divine images

## Historical Usage Timeline

### Ancient Near East Context

**Idol/statue usage:**
- Most common usage in ancient world = physical images
- Kings set up statues of themselves (representing presence)
- Temples contained divine images

**Genesis 1:26-27 radical redefinition:**
- **Humanity** is the image, not a statue
- **Living** image, not stone/metal
- **Universal** - all humans, not just king

### Biblical Development

**Genesis 1:26-27 (Primeval):**
- Humanity created in God's image
- Basis for human dignity
- Foundation for dominion mandate

**Genesis 5:3 (Genealogy):**
- Adam fathers Seth "in his own likeness (demut), after his image (tselem)"
- Parallel structure confirms semantic range
- Image passed down through generations

**Genesis 9:6 (Noah covenant):**
- "Whoever sheds man's blood, by man shall his blood be shed"
- "For God made man in his own image"
- **Ethical implication:** Image = basis for capital punishment

**Psalm 73:20 (Wisdom):**
- "As a dream when one awakes, so you despise their image"
- Fleeting nature of earthly power
- Contrast with true divine reality

**Ezekiel (Prophetic):**
- Used for visionary images (divine throne)
- Apocalyptic imagery

## Ancient Translations

### Septuagint (LXX)
**εἰκών** (eikon) - "image"
- Standard Greek translation
- Philosophical concept: form, representation
- Used for statues and Genesis 1:26

**ὁμοίωσις** (homoiosis) - "likeness"
- Genesis 1:26: κατ' εἰκόνα (according to image)
- Distinction from εἰκών maintained

**Theological development:**
- New Testament: Christ as εἰκών (Colossians 1:15)
- Humanity renewed in εἰκών (Colossians 3:10)

### Vulgate
**imaginem** - "image"
- Latin root of English "image"
- ad imaginem et similitudinem - "to image and likeness"

### Targum Onkelos
**בִּדְמוּת** (bidmut) - "in likeness"
- Aramaic avoids physical connotations
- Interpretive translation

## Theological Interpretations

### Jewish Perspectives

**Classical Rabbinic:**
- **Rationality:** Image = intellect, reason, moral capacity
- **Physical form debated:** 
  - Some: Body shape reflects divine (anthropomorphic)
  - Others: Non-corporeal aspects only

**Medieval:**
- **Maimonides:** Image = intellectual apprehension
  - Not physical (God has no body)
  - Highest human capacity
  
- **Nachmanides:** Image = life force, vitality
  - Connection to divine breath (Genesis 2:7)

**Modern Jewish:**
- **Tselem Elohim** = inherent human dignity
- Basis for human rights
- Universal equality (all humans bear image)

### Christian Perspectives

**Patristic:**
- **Irenaeus:** Image = rationality, free will
  - Likeness = spiritual growth
  - Image innate, likeness developed
  
- **Augustine:** Image = memory, intellect, will (trinitarian structure)
  - Trinity reflected in human psychology

**Reformation:**
- **Luther:** Image = righteousness and holiness (lost in Fall)
- **Calvin:** Image = wisdom, righteousness, holiness
  - Restored in Christ

**Modern:**
- **Substantive view:** Image = certain qualities (reason, morality)
- **Relational view:** Image = capacity for relationship with God
- **Functional view:** Image = representing God on earth (dominion)

**New Testament Development:**
- **Christ as Image:** Colossians 1:15 - "image of invisible God"
- **Renewal:** Colossians 3:10 - "renewed in knowledge after image of Creator"
- **Restoration:** Romans 8:29 - "conformed to image of his Son"

## Key Distinctions

### Image (צֶלֶם) vs. Likeness (דְּמוּת)

**Genesis 1:26 uses both:**
- בְּצַלְמֵנוּ (bǝ-tsalmenu) - "in our image"
- כִּדְמוּתֵנוּ (ki-demutenu) - "according to our likeness"

**Possible distinctions:**
1. **Tselem** = concrete representation
   **Demut** = abstract similarity

2. **Tselem** = exact representation
   **Demut** = general resemblance

3. **Both synonymous** - Hebrew poetic parallelism

**Scholarly consensus:** Some distinction intended, but exact nuance debated

### Image of God in Genesis vs. Ancient Near East

| ANE Practice | Genesis Distinction |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Idols represent gods | Humans represent God |
| King only bears image | All humans bear image |
| Static statues | Living beings |
| Local temples | Universal humanity |

**Revolutionary claim:** Every human is a "temple" of God's presence

## Ethical Implications

### Genesis 9:6 - Capital Punishment Basis

"Whoever sheds the blood of man, by man shall his blood be shed, for God made man in his own image."

**Logic:**
- Murder violates image of God
- Attack on humanity = attack on God's representative
- Severest penalty appropriate

### Modern Applications

**Human dignity:**
- All humans have inherent worth (not earned)
- Basis for human rights (UN Declaration)
- Opposition to slavery, discrimination

**Medical ethics:**
- Image-bearers deserve care
- Life has sacred value
- Euthanasia/abortion debates

**Environmental stewardship:**
- Humans represent God to creation
- Dominion = responsibility, not exploitation
- Care for earth as God's creation

## Translation Decision

### Options:

1. **"Image"** (traditional)
   - Preserves Hebrew term
   - Biblical precedent (Genesis 1:27, 9:6)

2. **"Likeness"** (alternative)
   - Emphasizes similarity not exact copy
   - Philosophical precision

3. **"Representation"** (functional)
   - Emphasizes representational role
   - Dominion context

### Recommended:

**"Image"** with footnote explaining:
- Not physical appearance
- Represents God's authority
- Basis for human dignity

### Expanded translation:

**Genesis 1:26-27:**
"Let us make humanity in our image [representation/likeness], according to our likeness; and let them have dominion... So God created humanity in his own image, in the image of God he created him; male and female he created them."
