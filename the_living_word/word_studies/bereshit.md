# Word Study: בְּרֵאשִׁית (bereshit)

**Lemma:** רֵאשִׁית (reshit)  
**Root:** ראש (rosh - head, chief, beginning)  
**Part of Speech:** Noun (feminine singular construct)  
**Occurrences:** 50x in Hebrew Bible  

---

## Morphological Analysis

**Form:** בְּרֵאשִׁית  
**Components:**
- בְּ (be) - preposition, "in/at"
- רֵאשִׁית (reshit) - noun, "beginning"

**Construct State:** The form רֵאשִׁית is construct (bound to following noun), but the MT vocalization makes it function as absolute "beginning of" rather than "in the beginning of [creation]."

**Vocalization Issue:**
- MT: בְּרֵאשִׁ֗ית (absolute-like reading)
- Alternative: בְּרֵאשִׁ֥ית (construct, requiring noun)

---

## Semantic Range

### Primary Meanings

1. **"Beginning, start" (temporal)**
   - Proverbs 8:22 - "YHWH created me as רֵאשִׁית of his way"
   - Jeremiah 2:3 - "firstfruits" (agricultural)

2. **"First, chief, best" (qualitative)**
   - Exodus 23:19 - "first of" (firstfruits)
   - Numbers 15:20 - "first of your dough"

3. **"Principal, most important"**
   - Micah 1:13 - "beginning of sin"
   - Ezekiel 40:1 - "beginning of the year"

### Usage Patterns

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Temporal | Beginning of time/event | Genesis 1:1, Psalm 111:10 |
| Agricultural | Firstfruits | Exodus 23:19 |
| Social | Chiefs/leaders | 1 Samuel 15:21 |
| Wisdom | Pre-eminence | Proverbs 8:22 |

---

## Historical Usage Timeline

### Earliest Known Usage
**Ugaritic:** rish (head, top) - cognate evidence

### Biblical Hebrew (Pre-exilic)
- Primarily construct: "beginning of [something]"
- Absolute rare before late biblical Hebrew

### Second Temple Period
- Expanded use in wisdom literature
- Proverbs 8:22 - personified Wisdom present at beginning
- Sirach 24:9 - Wisdom created "in the beginning"

### New Testament Usage
- John 1:1 - ἐν ἀρχῇ (en archē) - "in beginning"
- Hebrews 1:10 - "in the beginning you laid the foundations"
- Revelation 3:14 - ἀρχή (archē) - "beginning of God's creation"

### Rabbinic Interpretation
- **Bereishit Rabbah:** Multiple interpretations:
  1. "In the beginning" - first thing created
  2. "With Wisdom" - Proverbs 8 connection
  3. "For Israel" - Israel = "beginning" of God's way

---

## Ancient Translations

### Septuagint (LXX)
**ἐν ἀρχῇ** (en archē)
- Accusative absolute: "in [the] beginning"
- Treats Hebrew as absolute, not construct
- No "of" in Greek

### Vulgate
**in principio**
- "In [the] beginning"
- Follows LXX interpretation

### Peshitta (Syriac)
**b'reshit**
- Direct transliteration
- Same ambiguity as Hebrew

### Targum Onkelos
**b'qadmin**
- "In former time" / "at first"
- Aramaic interpretation

### Samaritan Pentateuch
**br'shyt** (no vowels)
- Consonantal identical to MT
- No vocalization to resolve ambiguity

## Theological Interpretations

### Jewish Perspectives

**Medieval:**
- **Rashi:** "In the beginning of God's creating" (construct)
- **Ibn Ezra:** Temporal absolute, not construct

**Modern Jewish:**
- NJPS adopts construct reading: "When God began to create"
- Emphasizes process over absolute beginning

### Christian Perspectives

**Patristic:**
- **Origen:** "In the beginning" = in the Son (Logos)
- **Augustine:** "In the beginning" = in Wisdom

**Reformation:**
- **Luther:** Absolute beginning, contra rabbinic interpretations
- **Calvin:** Temporal absolute, creation from nothing implied

**Modern:**
- Debate between absolute temporal and construct readings
- Some adopt NJPS construct for theological neutrality

## Textual Variants

### Masoretic Text (MT)
- Vocalization supports absolute reading
- Accents (ta'amim) confirm interpretation
- **Kethib/Qere:** None (only one reading)

### Qumran (4QGen-b)
- Fragments confirm consonantal text
- No vowels preserved in DSS
- No significant variants

### Septuagint Variants
- Some MSS: ἐν κεφαλαίῳ ("in the head/heading")
- Minority reading, not significant

## Concept Connections

### Related Concepts

**תֵּבֵל (tevel) - world/ cosmos**
- 1 Samuel 2:8 - "foundations of תֵּבֵל"
- Different from אֶרֶץ (eretz) - land/earth

**עוֹלָם (olam) - age/eternity**
- Temporal duration vs. spatial "beginning"
- Psalm 90:2 - "from olam to olam"

**קֶדֶם (qedem) - east/ancient time**
- Micah 5:2 - "from qedem"
- Spatial metaphor for temporal priority

### Intertextual Network

**Proverbs 8:22-31**
- Wisdom present at רֵאשִׁית
- Parallel to Genesis 1
- Possible allusion in John 1:1

**Sirach 24:9**
- Wisdom created "before ages"
- Expands Genesis concept

**Colossians 1:18**
- Messiah = "beginning" (archē)
- "Firstborn from the dead"
- Parallel archetype language

## Translation Decision

### Recommended Rendering

**Layer 6 (Literal):**
"In the beginning"

**Layer 7 (Expanded):**
"In [the] beginning [of God's creative activity]," or  
"When [the] Elohim [divine powers] began to create,"

### Rationale

1. **MT vocalization favors absolute:** The Masoretic pointing suggests absolute reading
2. **LXX precedent:** Ancient translation tradition supports absolute
3. **Ambiguity preserved:** Both meanings may be intentional
4. **Construct plausible:** Alternative reading should be footnoted

### Confidence Classification

**Original Meaning:** **Probable** - "In the beginning" (absolute) or "When God began" (construct)

**Certainty Level:** Cannot be definitively resolved
**Recommendation:** Preserve ambiguity in translation, note alternative in commentary

---

## What Is Lost in Translation

### Lost in English "In the beginning":

1. **Construct/absolute ambiguity** - Hebrew plays on both meanings
2. **Reshit as "firstfruits"** - agricultural connotation
3. **Proverbs 8 allusion** - wisdom theology connection
4. **Rabbinic multi-valence** - Jewish interpretive tradition

### Lost in "When God began":

1. **Absolute temporal** - "the" beginning suggests uniqueness
2. **Philosophical resonance** - "in the beginning" as technical term
3. **New Testament echoes** - John 1:1 formula
4. **Liturgical familiarity** - traditional phrasing
