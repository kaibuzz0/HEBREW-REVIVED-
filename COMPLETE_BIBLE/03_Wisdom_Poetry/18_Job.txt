================================================================================
THE BOOK OF 18 JOB
================================================================================

There was a man in the land of Uz whose name was Job, and that man was blameless and upright, one who feared God and turned away from evil... Then Satan answered the LORD and said, Does Job fear God for no reason?... And the LORD said to Satan, Behold, all that he has is in your hand. Only against him do not stretch out your hand.

Again there was a day when the sons of God came to present themselves before the LORD... And Satan answered the LORD and said, Skin for skin! All that a man has he will give for his life. But stretch out your hand and touch his bone and his flesh, and he will curse you to your face.

For I know that my Redeemer lives, and at the last he will stand upon the earth. And after my skin has been thus destroyed, yet in my flesh I shall see God, whom I shall see for myself, and my eyes shall behold, and not another.

Then the LORD answered Job out of the whirlwind and said, Who is this that darkens counsel by words without knowledge? Dress for action like a man; I will question you, and you make it known to me. Where were you when I laid the foundation of the earth? Tell me, if you have understanding.

I know that you can do all things, and that no purpose of yours can be thwarted... After the LORD had spoken these words to Job, the LORD restored the fortunes of Job, when he had prayed for his friends. And the LORD gave Job twice as much as he had before.

21 Naked I came from my mother's womb, and naked shall I return. The LORD gave, and the LORD has taken away; blessed be the name of the LORD.

25 For I know that my Redeemer lives, and at the last he will stand upon the earth.

5 I had heard of you by the hearing of the ear, but now my eye sees you.

[End of 18 Job]
