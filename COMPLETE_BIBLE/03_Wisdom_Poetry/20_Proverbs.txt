================================================================================
THE BOOK OF 20 PROVERBS
================================================================================

The proverbs of Solomon, son of David, king of Israel: To know wisdom and instruction, to understand words of insight... The fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge; fools despise wisdom and instruction.

Trust in the LORD with all your heart, and do not lean on your own understanding. In all your ways acknowledge him, and he will make straight your paths.

Above all else, guard your heart, for everything you do flows from it.

The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom, and knowledge of the Holy One is understanding.

The mouth of the righteous is a fountain of life.

A false balance is an abomination to the LORD, but a just weight is his delight.

A gentle tongue is a tree of life, but perverseness in it breaks the spirit.

Whoever walks with the wise becomes wise, but the companion of fools will suffer harm.

The heart of him who has understanding seeks knowledge, but the mouths of fools feed on folly.

A soft answer turns away wrath, but a harsh word stirs up anger.

Commit your work to the LORD, and your plans will be established.

A friend loves at all times, and a brother is born for adversity.

Death and life are in the power of the tongue.

Better is a poor person who walks in his integrity than one who is crooked in speech and is a fool.

Train up a child in the way he should go; even when he is old he will not depart from it.

A word fitly spoken is like apples of gold in a setting of silver.

Iron sharpens iron, and one man sharpens another.

The wicked flee when no one pursues, but the righteous are bold as a lion.

Where there is no prophetic vision the people cast off restraint, but blessed is he who keeps the law.

An excellent wife who can find? She is far more precious than jewels... Charm is deceitful, and beauty is vain, but a woman who fears the LORD is to be praised.
